Check Out the Trails

The race has three different trails each of varying difficulty. All of the trails begins and ends at the central camping village. The race will be run relay style in 24 legs. Each of the 8 runners on a team will run every one of the trails once. Runners must remain in the same order throughout the entire race. The team runs the loops in order collectively, but each runner will have a different pattern. The trails will total 15.7 miles for each runner.

The Green Trail: 3.6 Miles

A winding and gently rolling trail, the green will be the easiest and shortest loop. Runners will enjoy scenic views as they jog along this lone trail around a small creek. Beware - it can be rocky and ends in a small climb.

The Yellow Trail: 6.2 Miles

Runners will be amazed by the views along this traditional Appalachian wooded trail. The trail will take you through a large grove of towering pine trees. Enjoy the run over soft pine needles while taking in the landscape views. Beware - there is a steep drop around near the middle of the trail. The final two miles wind along the mountains.

The Red Trail: 6.2 Miles

While the red loop is the most difficult of the race, it is also a favorite of many runners. The trail weaves through tall ferns, twists over bolders, runs along a stream, and crosses over a swamp. All of the thrills and challenges of Appalachian running will be experienced in this trail.
